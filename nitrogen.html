Nitrogen

Element Overview

Atomic Number: 7

Atomic Mass: 14 u

Symbol: N

Classification: Nonmetal

Family Name: Nitrogen family

Period: 2

Physical Properties

State at Room Temperature: Gas

Color and Appearance: Colorless, odorless, and tasteless gas

Density: 1.2506 g/L at 0°C

Melting Point: 210.0 °C

Boiling Point: 195.8 °C

Chemical Properties

Common Compounds: •Ammonia (NH₃) • Nitric acid (HNO₃) • Nitrogen dioxide (NO₂) • Dinitrogen tetroxide (N₂O₄) • Nitrous oxide (N₂O)

Reactivity: • Relatively unreactive at room temperature due to its strong triple bond (N≡N). • Can react under high pressure or temperature, especially with hydrogen or oxygen.

Uses: • Fertilizers (via ammonia compounds) • Food packaging (to prevent spoilage) • Cryogenics (as liquid nitrogen) • In the production of explosives • Used to create an inert atmosphere for electronics and metal production.

Interesting Facts

Discovery: Discovered by Scottish physician Daniel Rutherford in 1772.

Unique Properties: Makes up about 78% of Earth’s atmosphere.
