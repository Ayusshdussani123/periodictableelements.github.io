Carbon

Element Overview

Atomic Number: 6

Atomic Mass: 12.01 u

Symbol: C

Classification: Nonmetal

Family Name: Carbon Family

Period: 2

Physical Properties

State at Room Temperature: Solid

Color and Appearance: Black (graphite) or transparent (diamond)

Density: 2.267 g/cm³ (graphite)

Melting Point: 3550 °C (graphite)

Boiling Point: 4027 °C (graphite)

Chemical Properties

Common Compounds: CO₂, CH₄, C₆H₁₂O₆

Reactivity: Reacts with oxygen to form CO₂, forms covalent bonds

Uses: Found in fuels, plastics, and organic compounds

Interesting Facts

Discovery: Known since ancient times

Unique Properties: Exists in multiple forms (allotropes) such as graphite, diamond, and graphene
